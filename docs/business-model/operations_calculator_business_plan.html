Business Plan & Growth Model · Rev. 2 · July 2026

Operations Utility Toolkit

An interconnected workbench of inventory and freight calculators for operators worldwide. The product is the suite, not any single tool. Traffic is sized on real worldwide search data. Return is driven by revenue per visitor and by converting repeat users into accounts, not by chasing raw pageviews.

1. Executive summary

This is a search-driven toolkit of operations calculators spanning two connected clusters: inventory planning and freight and dimensional weight. The strategy rests on three facts established from real Semrush worldwide data and the 2026 search landscape.

The suite is the moat, not any one calculator. No single tool carries the business. A reorder-point user needs safety stock next, then EOQ, then carrying cost. A CBM user needs chargeable weight and pallet utilisation. Interlinked tools raise pages per session, lengthen visits, and build the repeat usage that converts into accounts. The value compounds across the set.

Interactive calculators resist AI Overviews. Informational pages that explain a formula lose 30 to 40 percent of their clicks to AI answers. Tools that perform the calculation show under 3 percent disruption, and AI assistants route users to named calculators. The workbench sits on the durable side of that divide.

The niche is winnable but small, so the return comes from value per visitor. Worldwide search volume across the whole English portfolio is about 334,000 per month. Difficulty is low across the niche, so ranking is achievable. Fifty million pageviews a year remains roughly 35 times beyond owning the entire worldwide niche at position one. Display advertising is a floor. Affiliate and lead-gen for freight and logistics software is the mid lever. A productised account layer, API access, bulk calculation, saved scenarios, is the pillar that raises the ceiling.

Brand direction (in progress). The toolkit will run as a standalone brand, separate from Contxtyfy, with a fast and utilitarian personality: clarity over cleverness, speed over polish, one connected workbench across inventory and freight. Naming and visual identity are being developed as the Phase 4 build begins.

2. The product: an operations workbench

The site answers the practical planning questions an operator faces in a working day, and it answers the next question before the user leaves. The two clusters interlock.

Inventory cluster

When to reorder, how much safety stock to hold, EOQ, inventory turnover, days of cover, carrying cost, stockout risk, ABC classification. These pages are corpus-grounded, low difficulty, and fast to build. They form the on-ramp and the internal-link spine.

Freight and dimensional cluster

Dimensional weight, volumetric weight, CBM, freight density, chargeable weight, pallet utilisation, carton and package volume, approximate freight class. These carry the traffic and the commercial intent. They also carry the sourcing risk, since carrier divisors, NMFC classes, and parcel girth limits are not in the corpus and need external verification.

Why the combined suite beats a portfolio of standalone tools

Cross-linking turns single-answer visits into sessions. A user who lands on the CBM calculator from search discovers chargeable weight, then freight density, then pallet utilisation. Each hop adds a pageview, deepens intent, and increases the chance of an account signup. The same internal-link graph that drives SEO authority is the graph that drives session depth and conversion. One system, three payoffs.

3. The 2026 search landscape

Two-thirds of Google searches now end without a click. When an AI Overview appears, organic click-through roughly halves, and the loss concentrates on informational content. Business-technology queries trigger AI Overviews 80 to 90 percent of the time, which is exactly the territory of formula-explanation pages.

Interactive tools are the exception. Calculators, configurators, and assessments show the lowest AI-Overview disruption of any content type, and AI assistants actively name and route to functional tools. The implication is direct: build the calculators to be genuinely excellent and fast, keep the explanatory prose tight and accurate, and treat the corpus grounding as a correctness and citation aid rather than a traffic driver.

6. Monetization: three pillars

The suite thesis changes the revenue model. A single tool monetises thinly through ads. A connected workbench monetises through depth and repeat use.

Pillar 1: Display advertising (the floor)

AdSense pays roughly 2 to 5 dollars per thousand pageviews, rising to 4 to 12 on Ezoic past 10,000 sessions a month, and 15 to 30 on Raptive past 50,000. The worldwide portfolio reaches the Ezoic tier at maturity in the Base case. Ads keep the lights on. They do not build the business, and heavy ad density fights the clean tool experience the audience expects.

Pillar 2: Affiliate and lead-gen (the mid lever)

The freight and dimensional audience is exactly who freight-quote platforms, 3PLs, WMS, ERP, and inventory-software vendors pay to reach. High CPCs in the data confirm the commercial intent: CBM at 14.83, pallet at 5.84, break-even at 5.91, chargeable weight at 4.01. A single high-value partnership at 50 to 200 dollars per qualified lead changes the economics far more than any amount of extra display traffic.

Pillar 3: Accounts, API, and bulk calculation (the ceiling-raiser)

This is where the suite earns its keep. Free tools draw the search traffic. The connected experience creates repeat use. Repeat use justifies an account, and the account unlocks value that a one-off visitor never needs:

  • Saved scenarios and history, so a planner returns to their own inputs.
  • Bulk calculation, running a spreadsheet of SKUs or shipments through a tool in one pass.
  • API access, for teams that want reorder points or chargeable weights inside their own systems.
  • Team and export features, shared results, branded CSV and PDF, seat-based plans.

The repo already points here: the README lists CSV and spreadsheet export as a Phase 1 deliverable, and the calculation library is a clean base for an API. This pillar carries real uncertainty, since free-calculator audiences convert to paid at low rates, and it only works if the account genuinely gates value people want. It is also the only pillar with recurring revenue and a ceiling well above the ad and affiliate lines.

How the suite improves the model, in numbers

Two assumptions move once the workbench is connected. Pages per session rises from about 1.3 for a standalone calculator to 1.9 across an interlinked set, which lifts ad and affiliate revenue proportionally. And a small account conversion compounds. The model runs conservative defaults: 1.5 percent of sessions create a free account, 3 percent of those pay a 19-dollar monthly plan, and 6 percent churn each month. At mature Base traffic near 11,700 sessions a month that settles at roughly 88 paying subscribers, about 20,000 dollars a year of recurring revenue, which on its own is several times the entire ads-plus-affiliate line. The account layer accumulates rather than resetting each month, so it keeps growing as traffic grows. It is also the single most uncertain input in the model. Halve the conversion and the account line roughly halves.

8. Google ranking and GEO plan

Technical foundation

  • One clear H1, unique title and meta description, canonical URL per tool page.
  • WebApplication schema on every calculator, plus FAQ and Breadcrumb schema. This tells Google and AI engines the page is a tool.
  • Formula, inputs, worked example, and result visible in the HTML, not hidden behind a JavaScript-only shell.
  • Fast mobile-first render, sitemap, robots, HTTPS, and the internal-link graph that connects related calculators.

On-page, built for humans and for citation

  • Lead with a direct answer capsule: the formula and a one-line reading of what the result means, extractable on its own.
  • Original worked examples with real numbers, which AI engines can cite and cannot fabricate.
  • Explicit assumptions, limitations, and a clear note to confirm carrier divisors and freight class with the carrier. This is both trust signal and honest practice.
  • Corpus-grounded explanations, which make the pages the source an assistant quotes.

Authority and links

A focused site ranks on topical depth and a few quality links. Being the free tool for a specific calculation earns links naturally. The cross-linked suite concentrates authority across both clusters.

10. Success metrics

  • Indexed pages and impressions in Search Console. The first proof of life.
  • Top-three rankings on freight class and the core cluster terms.
  • Pages per session. The direct measure of whether the suite thesis is working.
  • Tool completion rate. The product-quality and citation-worthiness signal.
  • Account signups and paid conversion. The ceiling-raiser.
  • Revenue per thousand visitors, blended across ads, affiliate, and accounts. The north star.
  • AI-citation share. Whether assistants name the tools.

11. Risks and honest bottom line

On ads and affiliate alone, worldwide, this is a low-thousands-per-year site. The niche is genuinely small, and no reworking of geography changes that. The case for building it rests on two things the standalone-calculator view misses: the suite lifts session depth and retention, and the account layer adds recurring revenue with a ceiling well above the ad line. Both require real execution. The account pillar in particular converts at low rates unless it gates value people want, and the freight reference data must be verified before those tools can be trusted. Treat the ad and affiliate revenue as the floor that funds the build, and treat accounts and partnerships as the reason the project is worth the effort.

12. Immediate next steps

  • Ship the inventory cluster from the existing specs and connect Search Console. Get real impression data.
  • Verify the freight reference tables, then build freight class and CBM as the traffic anchors.
  • Design the account layer early, even if built later, so tools capture inputs in a shape that supports save, bulk, and API.
  • Wire an account and subscription line into the financial model, and raise pages-per-session to reflect the connected suite.
  • Open conversations with one or two freight and logistics software partners once the freight pages have traffic.
