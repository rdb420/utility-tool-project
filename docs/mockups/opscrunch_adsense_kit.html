01   The principle

Ads are the floor

The model is clear about where money comes from. Accounts and affiliate carry the business; display ads fund the build and little more. So ads are placed to a strict hierarchy, and they always yield to the tool and to the higher-value calls to action.

AccountsRecurring, the ceiling. Protect first.
Affiliate / lead-genThe mid lever. Never crowded by ads.
Display adsThe floor. Fits around everything above.
The golden rule. An ad never touches the tool. Nothing renders between the inputs and the readout, nothing sits above the tool on the first screen, and nothing ever overlaps the Crunch button. The calculator loads and works before any ad script runs.

Who sees ads

Ad eligibility is set by account tier. Signing up is free and stays ad-supported, so the free tool and the free account both still earn display revenue. Removing ads is a paid perk, one more concrete reason to subscribe alongside the API, bulk calculation, and saved scenarios.

Why this works. Paid subscribers are a small share of traffic, so the display revenue given up is negligible, while an ad-free experience is a tangible upgrade that lifts paid conversion. The funnel still monetises at every step: anonymous and free-account visitors fund the floor, and the people who value the tools enough to return pay to lose the ads and unlock bulk and API.

04   Placement rules

Do and do not

  • Label every unit with the word Advertisement in the mono micro-label style, so ads read as ads.
  • Keep ads visually distinct from OpsCrunch components. No signal orange, no amber, no readout styling. An ad must never look like a result.
  • Reserve space for every slot so the page never shifts when an ad loads.
  • Keep more content than ads on every page. The formula, example, assumptions, and FAQ carry the weight.
  • No ad between inputs and readout, and none above the tool on the first screen.
  • No ad within a thumb's reach of the Crunch button or any control, which prevents accidental clicks and the policy strike that follows.
  • No auto-ads inside the tool zone. Use manual placements and exclude the calculator region, so Google cannot inject a unit into the workbench.
  • No large sticky ads, prestitials with countdowns, or sound. These fail the Better Ads Standards and drive people off.
  • No more than one ad without content between it and the next. Two stacked ad units never appear together.
No pop-ups. The only overlay OpsCrunch shows is the privacy and consent or cookies banner on first visit, which the EEA, UK, and Switzerland require anyway. There are no newsletter modals, no interstitials or prestitials, no exit-intent pop-ups, and no sign-up dialogs. Upsells stay inline, like the dashed bulk nudge, never modal. The one overlay that is not a pop-up is the dismissible mobile anchor (Slot M): it is a sticky ad, not a modal, and it never covers the tool.
05   Performance

Ads never slow the crunch

  • Calculator first. The tool's own script loads and runs independently. If ad code fails or is blocked, the calculator still works.
  • Reserve height on every container to hold Cumulative Layout Shift near zero. The numbers in the slot spec are minimums.
  • Lazy-load everything below the first screen (B, C, D), so ads fetch as the reader scrolls, not on load.
  • Load ad script async and after the tool is interactive, protecting Largest Contentful Paint and Interaction to Next Paint.
  • Never let an ad unit block rendering of the tool or the formula content.
06   Policy compliance

Pass review, stay approved

  • Valuable content on every page. The working tool plus the formula, worked example, and FAQ satisfy the content requirement. Never publish a bare calculator with no explanation.
  • Publish ads.txt at the domain root listing authorised sellers, to protect revenue from spoofed inventory.
  • Label ads only as Advertisement or Sponsored Links, the only wording AdSense permits.
  • Keep the legal pages live: Privacy Policy, Cookie Policy, Terms, Contact, About. These are approval and trust requirements.
  • Never encourage clicks with wording, arrows, or placement that invites accidental taps.
  • No ads on thin, error, or empty-state pages. If there is no content, there is no ad.
07   Consent and privacy

The launch blocker for your traffic

OpsCrunch traffic is heavily EU, UK, and international, which makes consent the first thing to get right, not the last. Without a compliant setup, Google stops serving ads to exactly the higher-value regions you most want.

Mandatory before any ad serves in Europe. A Google-certified Consent Management Platform integrated with IAB TCF v2.2, plus Google Consent Mode v2, is required to serve ads to the EEA and UK (since 16 January 2024) and Switzerland (since 31 July 2024). No certified CMP means no ads to those users.
  • Choose a certified CMP. Google's own Privacy and Messaging European regulations message is free and certified, or use a certified third party. Confirm it is on Google's current certified list before committing.
  • Implement Consent Mode v2 (the ad_user_data and ad_personalization signals) and block ad scripts until consent is granted for users in covered regions.
  • Cover the other regions too. A California privacy-choices link for CPRA, and an Australian-law-aware privacy and cookie policy, since the founder and a share of traffic are Australian.
  • Detect region so the consent banner shows where it is required and stays out of the way where it is not.
  • Do not serve personalised ads without a valid consent signal in the EEA, UK, or Switzerland. It is both a policy breach and a revenue risk.

Regulations shift, so verify the current requirements and the certified-CMP list against Google's own help pages before launch rather than treating this page as final.

Read this honestly. The model shows this niche may sit in the AdSense and Ezoic tiers for a long time. That means piling on ad density to chase display RPM is the wrong trade, because it costs tool completion and affiliate clicks, which are worth far more per visitor. Move up a tier when session volume clears the gate, not by crowding the page. Ezoic in particular will want to place more ads than this kit allows, so hold the line on the no-ad zone.

Guardrail metrics

Watch these together, never RPM alone. If added ad density lifts RPM but drops tool completion, affiliate click-through, or Core Web Vitals, roll it back. Ads serve the floor; they must not erode the ceiling.

09   Implementation

The ad-slot component

One reusable slot: a reserved-height container, a mono Advertisement label, consent-gated and lazy where required, styled to look nothing like an OpsCrunch component.

<!-- reserved height prevents layout shift -->
<aside class="ad-slot" style="min-height:280px" data-lazy="true">
  <span class="ad-label">Advertisement</span>
  <ins class="adsbygoogle" …></ins>
</aside>

/* paid subscriber? render no slot and make no ad request at all */
/* only after: consent granted (region-aware) AND tool interactive */
/* auto-ads: page-level exclusion over the .oc-tool region */
Order of operations. Render the calculator, make it interactive, resolve consent, then request ads into the reserved slots. Never the reverse.
Gate by tier. Before requesting any ad, check subscription status. For a paid subscriber, skip the slot entirely rather than hiding it, so no request is made and the page is genuinely ad-free. Anonymous and free-account visitors follow the normal consent-then-serve path.
