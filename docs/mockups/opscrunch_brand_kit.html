01   Logo

Wordmark

Lowercase, heavy grotesk, tight tracking. ops in the surface colour, crunch in signal orange, closed by a blinking amber block cursor. The cursor is the brand's tell: a terminal waiting to crunch a number. It blinks only where motion is welcome, and holds steady when reduced motion is set.

App mark

For favicons, avatars, and tight spaces: a rounded ink tile holding the amber cursor. It reads as OpsCrunch shrunk to its signature. A signal-orange variant exists for a single accent moment, never as the default.

Clearspace & size

Keep clearspace of one cursor-width on every side. Minimum wordmark width 96px on screen. Do not set below 22px cap height.

Do

  • Keep the wordmark lowercase and one weight.
  • Use the light lockup on paper, the dark lockup on ink.
  • Let the cursor be the only ornament.

Do not

  • Recolour "crunch" to anything but signal orange.
  • Set the wordmark in a serif, or add a tagline lockup.
  • Stretch, outline, or drop-shadow the mark.
02   Colour

03   Typography

Two roles, one rule

Grotesk sans for everything you read, drawn from the system stack so pages load instantly with no font fetch. A monospace utility face for everything you count: every input, figure, result, and label. Numbers in mono line up like an instrument, which is the point.

04   Voice

Plain verbs, sentence case, no filler. A control says exactly what it does and keeps the same word through the whole flow. Notes are honest about limits, because that is how a free tool earns an operator's trust. The tone is a competent colleague at the next desk, not a mascot and not an enterprise brochure.

Interface vocabulary

Primary action is always Crunch it. Save/bulk actions read Save & run bulk. Copy actions are Copy result and Copy full. Disclaimers open with the fact, then the caveat: state the formula, state the assumption, then say when to confirm with a carrier, accountant, or broker.

05   Motion
  • Result flash. When a value updates, the readout pulses amber once over 0.5s. This is the one orchestrated moment, the "crunch."
  • Cursor blink. The wordmark cursor blinks on a 1.15s step, evoking a terminal ready for input.
  • Hover. Buttons deepen to signal-press; tiles lift with a soft signal-tinted shadow. Fast, 150ms.
  • Restraint. Nothing else animates. No scroll parallax, no entrance choreography. Speed is the brand, so the interface never makes anyone wait for a flourish.
  • Reduced motion. Under prefers-reduced-motion, the flash and blink stop and the interface stays completely still.
06   Accessibility floor
  • Contrast. Body text meets WCAG AA on paper and ink. Amber is used for large readout digits on ink only, never small text on light.
  • Focus. Every control shows a visible focus ring: 3px amber on the primary button, signal on inputs.
  • Keyboard. The whole calculator works without a mouse. Enter crunches. Toggles are real buttons with aria-pressed.
  • Targets. Tap targets are at least 44px on touch. Inputs are large and mono for legibility.
  • Labels. Every input has a programmatic label; the tool is usable with a screen reader.
