| NMFC/LTL         | NMFTA confirms freight classes range **50 to 500** and classification is based on density, handling, stowability, and liability. NMFTA/NMFC 2025 changes expand the FCDC density scale to **13 subprovisions**, effective **2025-07-19**.                                  |

What changed or needs special handling:

* **USPS changes on 2026-07-12**, after this pack date: DIM divisor changes from **166 to 139**, and fractional dimensions round **up**.
* **NMFC Docket 2025-1** moved many items toward the **13-sub density structure**, effective **2025-07-19**; ClassIT+ is the official lookup path.
* **TNT Australia has an internal public-page conflict**: `m3 x 200` appears in service rows, while domestic volumetric calculation says `cm3 / 4000`, i.e. `m3 x 250`. Treat this as service-specific until confirmed by current contract/rate guide.
* **FedEx rounding conflict:** current FedEx official page says nearest whole inch; some secondary sources claim round-up rules from 2025. Do not ingest the secondary claim without a current FedEx service-guide confirmation.

Ingest first:

1. UPS official shipping dimensions and weight page.
2. FedEx official DIM weight page and DIM calculator.
3. Australia Post July 2026 Post Charges Guide and size/weight page.
4. NMFTA NMFC overview, NMFTA Help Center density FAQ, and NMFTA Docket 2025-1 disposition bulletin.
5. USPS Federal Register final rule for future-effective 2026-07-12 DIM changes.

Required caveats:

* Carrier DIM divisors and cubic conversion factors are subject to change and may differ by country, service, rate card, tariff, account agreement, and date.
* NMFC freight class is not determined by density alone in all cases; handling, stowability, liability, commodity description, packaging, and carrier inspection can affect classification.
* Public freight-class calculators are estimators and should not replace NMFTA ClassIT+, official NMFC data, carrier tariffs, or contracted rate advice.
* Formulas are generally stable, but divisor constants and freight-class mappings must be stored as dated reference data.
